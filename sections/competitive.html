Competitive Landscape

Multi-agent frameworks battle for developer mindshare as MetaGPT and AutoGen dominate stars, while release velocity signals market momentum

COMPETITIVE

MetaGPT dominates open-source multi-agent space with 67K stars

MetaGPT has emerged as the leading open-source multi-agent framework with 67,173 stars, representing a multi-agent software company simulation that allows multiple AI agents to collaborate on complex software development tasks.

The framework's architecture enables role-based agent specializations, making it particularly attractive for developers building sophisticated automation pipelines.

Its v0.8.2 release indicates active maintenance and feature development, positioning it as a top contender against proprietary alternatives.

COMPETITIVE

Microsoft's AutoGen bridges enterprise credibility with 57K stars

Microsoft's AutoGen framework has accumulated 57,168 stars, making it the second-largest community in the multi-agent space.

The python-v0.7.5 release demonstrates Microsoft's commitment to enterprise-grade AI agent development with strong documentation and corporate backing.

AutoGen's multi-agent conversational architecture enables complex workflows while leveraging Microsoft's ecosystem integration advantages.

COMPETITIVE

CrewAI surges to 49K stars as developer favorite for multi-agent orchestration

CrewAI has reached 49,087 stars with its 1.14.2rc1 release, establishing itself as a popular choice for developers building multi-agent systems.

The framework's strength lies in its role-based agent architecture that simplifies creating teams of AI agents with distinct responsibilities.

Its relatively lower barrier to entry compared to other frameworks makes it a strong competitor for projects requiring quick implementation.

Phidata's tool-first approach differentiates at 39K stars

Phidata has built a following of 39,495 stars through its agent framework with integrated tools approach.

The v2.5.17 release shows consistent development focused on giving AI agents practical capabilities to interact with external systems.

This tool-centric philosophy appeals to developers building agents that need to execute real-world actions beyond pure reasoning.

The framework excels at building stateful, cyclical workflows that many agent systems require, leveraging the broader LangChain ecosystem.

This architectural differentiation attracts developers already invested in the LangChain universe.
