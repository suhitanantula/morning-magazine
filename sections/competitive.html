Multi-agent AI frameworks intensify open-source race as Microsoft, Meta, and specialized players compete for developer adoption

COMPETITIVE

MetaGPT and AutoGen dominate open-source multi-agent race with 67K+ combined stars

MetaGPT leads the competitive landscape with 67,364 stars as a multi-agent software company simulation framework, while Microsoft's AutoGen follows closely at 57,353 stars.

These two frameworks represent the highest community engagement in the multi-agent AI space, signaling strong developer preference for production-ready agent orchestration tools.

The star velocity indicates sustained interest as organizations evaluate agent frameworks for enterprise deployment.

CrewAI surges to 49K stars, capturing developer mindshare in agent framework market

CrewAI has emerged as a dominant player in the multi-agent framework space with 49,652 stars, surpassing LangGraph (30,125 stars) and Phidata (39,623 stars).

Its recent release of version 1.14.3a3 demonstrates active development cadence.

CrewAI's popularity stems from its intuitive role-based agent design, making it accessible to developers new to multi-agent systems while remaining powerful enough for production workloads.
