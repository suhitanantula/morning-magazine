Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer Created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically created for AI leadership, indicating strategic prioritisation of AI transformation at board level

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive recruitment posting

Why powerful: A dedicated C-suite role signals board-level commitment and dedicated budget. Creates internal sponsor for AI initiatives and signals the organisation is treating AI as a strategic priority, not a tactical experiment

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did you create the CAIO role and what mandate did the board give them?
  • 🔍 What resources has the board allocated to support the CAIO in driving transformation?
  • 🔍 How is the CAIO measuring success of AI initiatives across the organisation?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-First Public Mandate

What it is: CEO or board has explicitly stated AI-first or AI transformation in public communications such as earnings calls, investor days, or press interviews

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, CEO interview, annual report CEO letter

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability. When CEOs announce AI priorities externally, they create internal pressure to deliver. This signals budget allocation and executive attention that BD opportunities can capitalise on

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What specific AI outcomes did the CEO commit to in the mandate?
  • 🔍 How has the board operationalised the AI-first mandate across business units?
  • 🔍 What's the timeline for delivering on the AI transformation commitment?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board Pressure / Board Directors Talking About AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy appearing in board materials, announcements, or individual director communications

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussions, board committee charters adding AI oversight

Why powerful: Board pressure forces executive action. When directors are actively discussing AI, they demand progress reports and budget justifications. This creates urgency and funding pipelines that external partners can access

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What AI topics has the board been discussing in recent meetings?
  • 🔍 What board-level governance structure exists for AI oversight?
  • 🔍 How frequently does the board receive AI transformation updates?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI Mandate

What it is: New CEO (appointed within 12-18 months) with a track record of AI transformation or who has signalled AI as a priority in their mandate

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO inaugural speech

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and lack legacy systems to protect. They bring mandate from the board to make changes and are often looking for external partners to accelerate delivery

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the new CEO's vision for AI transformation?
  • 🔍 What legacy systems is the new leadership looking to transform?
  • 🔍 Does the CEO have a preferred model for engaging external AI partners?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI Platform Team Building

What it is: Organisation building internal AI infrastructure (10+ engineers on RAG, agents, embeddings pipelines) rather than buying off-the-shelf solutions

Examples: Engineering blog posts, job postings for platform engineers, GitHub contributions, tech conference talks

Why powerful: Building internal capability signals serious investment and long-term commitment. These teams need strategic partners for guidance, accelerate delivery, and help navigate vendor selection for components they don't want to build

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving your decision to build versus buy AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 What does your internal AI platform roadmap look like over the next 18 months?
  • 🔍 Where do you see gaps between your build ambitions and current capacity?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI Talent Hiring Spree

What it is: Significant surge in AI-specific hires (5+ roles in 6 months) including prompt engineers, AI architects, and ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, recruitment announcements

Why powerful: Hiring sprees indicate scale-up phase. These new hires often bring fresh perspectives and mandate to change existing processes. They typically need external support to accelerate time-to-value and navigate vendor landscape

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving the surge in AI hiring - new initiatives or replacing capacity?
  • 🔍 What skill gaps are you hiring to address?
  • 🔍 How are you integrating new AI hires with existing technology teams?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process Redesign Evidence

What it is: Evidence of eliminated old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog posts, case studies, press mentions, internal process documentation

Why powerful: Process redesign signals transformation beyond experimentation. When companies redesign workflows around AI, they're committing to structural change - creating opportunities for strategic partners who can advise on transformation methodology

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which processes have you redesigned around AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 What's the methodology you're using for process redesign?
  • 🔍 What resistance are you encountering when eliminating legacy workflows?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI Integrator Partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators (not just buying SaaS tools) to drive transformation

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, SI engagement announcements

Why powerful: Engaging integrators signals willingness to invest in transformation, not just tools. These partnerships indicate the company recognises they need external expertise - a natural fit for Helix Lab's strategic positioning

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What AI consultancy or integrator partners are you working with?
  • 🔍 What has been the outcome of those partnerships so far?
  • 🔍 What's your model for deciding what to build internally versus partner on?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI Budget Increase

What it is: Year-over-year increase of 50%+ in AI budget

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, internal budget approvals

Why powerful: Budget increases create immediate opportunity windows. When AI budgets surge, organisations need to deploy capital quickly, creating demand for partners who can accelerate delivery

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What drove the 50%+ increase in AI budget this year?
  • 🔍 How is the budget allocated across build vs buy vs partner?
  • 🔍 What's your timeline for deploying the increased budget?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government Policy Signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements that affect the company's sector

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Government action creates urgency and forces companies to respond. Policy shifts often open funding opportunities and compliance requirements that drive transformation timelines, creating BD opportunities with tight windows

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is recent government AI policy affecting your sector strategy?
  • 🔍 What compliance or regulatory requirements are driving your AI timeline?
  • 🔍 Are you accessing any government funding or incentives for AI transformation?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry Disruption Indicators

What it is: Company's sector experiencing AI-driven disruption from new entrants, margin compression, or customer behaviour shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, increasing industry analyst coverage, market research

Why powerful: Disruption creates urgency even for companies not yet transforming. When sectors face competitive pressure from AI-native entrants, companies that were previously conservative become motivated buyers. This context helps prioritise leads

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is AI changing the competitive dynamics in your sector?
  • 🔍 What new AI-native competitors have emerged in your market?
  • 🔍 How are customer expectations shifting based on AI-driven alternatives?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI Moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI advantage

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, analyst comparisons

Why powerful: Competitor activity creates FOMO-driven urgency. When competitors announce AI wins, companies that were in 'wait and see' mode suddenly need to respond. This is powerful for overcoming internal resistance to transformation investment

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which competitors do you see pulling ahead through AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 What competitive gaps are you trying to close with AI?
  • 🔍 How are you benchmarking your AI maturity against competitors?
