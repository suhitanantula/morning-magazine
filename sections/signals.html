Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically for AI created to lead transformation initiatives

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive recruitment notice

Why powerful: Signals structural commitment at the highest level - the company is organizing for AI transformation, not just experimenting

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did you create the CAIO role and what mandate did they receive?
  • 🔍 What is the CAIO's budget and reporting line?
  • 🔍 How many people now report to the AI leadership team?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO or board explicitly stated AI as strategic priority in public communications

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, annual report letter

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability and signal resource allocation is coming - executives rarely make public statements without following through

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Can you share what the CEO said about AI in your last earnings call?
  • 🔍 What did the board communicate about AI strategy at your last AGM?
  • 🔍 How is the AI mandate reflected in your corporate strategy document?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure on AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy appearing in board materials or director communications

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussions, board subcommittee creation

Why powerful: Board pressure forces executive action - when directors are asking questions, CEOs must deliver answers and results

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How frequently is AI discussed in your board meetings?
  • 🔍 Which board directors are driving the AI conversation?
  • 🔍 What AI initiatives are included in your board materials?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with track record of AI transformation or who has signaled AI as priority

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO vision statement

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and have no legacy systems to protect - they are looking to make their mark

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What was the incoming CEO's approach to AI in their previous role?
  • 🔍 What mandate did the board give the new CEO around AI?
  • 🔍 How has the leadership change affected technology investment priorities?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team

What it is: Company building internal AI infrastructure rather than just buying SaaS tools

Examples: Job postings for 10+ engineers, engineering blog posts, LinkedIn about RAG/agents/embeddings pipelines

Why powerful: Building not buying signals long-term commitment and sophistication - they are investing in capabilities, not one-off tools

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What internal AI platform capabilities are you building?
  • 🔍 How many engineers are dedicated to your AI infrastructure?
  • 🔍 What does your AI tech stack look like internally?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: Significant increase in AI-specific hires including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge (5+ AI roles in 6 months), recruitment campaigns

Why powerful: Talent acquisition is a leading indicator of intent - companies don't hire AI talent without having problems to solve and projects planned

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How many AI roles have you hired in the last 6 months?
  • 🔍 What AI capabilities are you building internally versus acquiring?
  • 🔍 What's driving your AI talent acquisition strategy?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Evidence of eliminated old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog posts, case studies, press mentions of workflow transformation, operational changes

Why powerful: Real transformation changes how work gets done - feature announcements are not transformation, but process redesign is

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which workflows have you redesigned around AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 How has AI changed your core business processes?
  • 🔍 What new AI-native processes have you created?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators rather than just buying SaaS tools

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, system integrator engagements

Why powerful: Engaging integrators signals complexity and commitment - companies invest in external expertise when they are serious about transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which AI partners or integrators are you working with?
  • 🔍 What kind of transformation engagement are you pursuing?
  • 🔍 How are you supplementing internal capability with external expertise?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: Material year-over-year increase in AI budget allocation

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, internal budget approvals, funding announcements

Why powerful: Budget is a concrete commitment - percentage increases show strategic prioritization and willingness to invest

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How has your AI budget changed year-over-year?
  • 🔍 What percentage of tech budget is allocated to AI?
  • 🔍 How is your AI investment funded - new money or reallocation?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements affecting the company's sector

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Policy creates urgency and forces companies to respond - it also often opens funding opportunities and changes competitive dynamics

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is your sector responding to recent government AI policy?
  • 🔍 What regulatory changes are driving your AI planning?
  • 🔍 Are you accessing any government AI funding or incentives?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: Company's sector undergoing AI-driven disruption from new entrants, margin compression, or customer behavior shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, increasing industry analyst coverage, market disruption news

Why powerful: External pressure creates urgency - companies in disrupted sectors must transform to survive, making them more receptive to BD conversations

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is AI disruption affecting your sector?
  • 🔍 What new competitors or business models are emerging?
  • 🔍 How are customer expectations changing in your industry?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI advantages

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, competitive intelligence reports

Why powerful: Competitive pressure motivates action - when competitors move, companies must respond to protect market position

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which competitors are leading on AI in your space?
  • 🔍 What competitive gaps are you seeing from AI adoption?
  • 🔍 How are you thinking about AI-enabled competitive advantage?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

New AI features announced

What it is: Product updates or feature releases with AI components, not organizational transformation

Examples: Product launch press releases, feature announcement blog posts, app updates

Why powerful: Low precision signal - product AI features don't necessarily mean the company is transforming its operations

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS

    Why powerful: Low precision signal - training alone is preparation, not transformation
