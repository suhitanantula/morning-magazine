Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer Created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically created for AI leadership, indicating formalised AI transformation commitment at board level

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive recruitment search

Why powerful: Creation of a dedicated C-suite role signals board-level commitment and structural reorganisation — this is not a pilot or experiment but an institutional mandate

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did you establish your Chief AI Officer role, and what mandate did the board give them?
  • 🔍 What reporting structure does your CAIO have — do they report directly to the CEO or board?
  • 🔍 What budget authority does your AI leader have, and how many teams report into them?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-First Public Mandate

What it is: CEO or board has explicitly stated AI as a strategic priority in public communications such as earnings calls, investor days, or press interviews

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, annual report letter from CEO

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability — CEOs who say 'AI-first' in front of investors have board and shareholder pressure to deliver, making them motivated buyers for strategic support

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did your CEO first publicly frame AI as a strategic priority? What was the context?
  • 🔍 What specific outcomes has the board said they expect from your AI transformation?
  • 🔍 How is AI performance being measured in executive scorecards?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board Pressure / Board Directors Talking About AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy appearing in board materials, with directors actively discussing AI initiatives

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussions, board strategy documents

Why powerful: Board pressure is the most powerful forcing function in enterprise — when directors ask about AI, CEOs must act. This creates urgency and budget availability that wouldn't exist otherwise

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How frequently is AI appearing on your board agenda now versus 12 months ago?
  • 🔍 What specific board directors have been driving the AI conversation?
  • 🔍 Has the board approved any AI-specific investment cases recently?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI Mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with either a track record of AI transformation or who has signalled AI as a priority in their mandate

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO vision statements

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have fresh board backing, and have no legacy systems or political relationships to protect — they are natural change agents

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did your new CEO join, and what did they say about AI in their onboarding?
  • 🔍 What was the CEO's track record with AI at their previous organisation?
  • 🔍 Has the board given the CEO specific AI transformation targets for their first 12-24 months?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI Platform Team Building Not Buying

What it is: Engineering team of 10+ building internal AI infrastructure including RAG systems, agents, and embedding pipelines

Examples: Job postings for ML engineers, engineering blog posts, LinkedIn profiles showing internal AI platform work, technical architecture announcements

Why powerful: Companies building their own AI platform are making a serious, long-term bet — they are investing in capabilities rather than point solutions, indicating transformation scope

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How large is your internal AI platform team, and what infrastructure are you building?
  • 🔍 What's driving your decision to build rather than buy AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 What does your AI platform roadmap look like for the next 18 months?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI Talent Hiring Spree

What it is: Significant hiring surge of 5+ AI-specific roles within 6 months including prompt engineers, AI architects, and ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, recruitment announcements, talent acquisition press releases

Why powerful: Hiring sprees indicate growth intent and resource commitment — they show the company is moving from planning to execution phase

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How many AI-focused hires have you made in the last 6 months? What roles?
  • 🔍 What's driving your AI hiring urgency? Is there a specific timeline?
  • 🔍 Are you hiring to build internal capability or to manage external partnerships?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process Redesign Evidence

What it is: Evidence of eliminated old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog posts about process changes, case studies, press mentions of workflow transformation, internal change management communications

Why powerful: Process redesign proves transformation is happening at the operational level — not just technology adoption but actual business model change

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which legacy processes have you recently retired or restructured due to AI?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring productivity gains from your process redesign?
  • 🔍 What AI-native processes have you created that didn't exist before?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI Integrator Partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators rather than just purchasing SaaS tools

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, integrator relationship disclosures, joint solution launches

Why powerful: Companies engaging integrators are tackling complex transformation that requires external expertise — they recognise they cannot do this alone

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which AI consulting or integration partners are you working with currently?
  • 🔍 What was the trigger that made you decide to bring in external AI expertise?
  • 🔍 How are you structuring your integrator relationships — project-based or ongoing?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI Budget Increase

What it is: Year-over-year increase of 50%+ in AI-specific budget

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, internal budget approvals, CFO communications about AI investment

Why powerful: Budget increases signal institutional commitment — 50%+ YOY growth means AI is moving from experimental to strategic investment

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's your YOY AI budget growth rate? What drove that increase?
  • 🔍 How is your AI budget structured — is it centrally managed or distributed to business units?
  • 🔍 What proportion of your total technology budget is allocated to AI?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government Policy Signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements that affect the company's sector, creating regulatory or market urgency

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Government policy creates urgency and forces companies to respond — it also often opens funding opportunities and shapes competitive dynamics

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is recent government AI policy affecting your sector?
  • 🔍 Are you pursuing any government AI incentives or funding programs?
  • 🔍 How is your compliance roadmap responding to emerging AI regulations?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry Disruption Indicators

What it is: Company's sector is undergoing AI-driven disruption through new entrants, margin compression, or customer behaviour shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, industry analyst coverage, market research on AI disruption

Why powerful: Disruption context helps prioritise leads — companies in disrupted sectors are more motivated to act and have clearer ROI cases for AI transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How do you see AI changing your industry over the next 3-5 years?
  • 🔍 What competitive threats are you seeing from AI-native entrants?
  • 🔍 Which of your traditional business models are most vulnerable to AI disruption?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI Moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI-powered capabilities

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, analyst comparisons

Why powerful: Competitor activity creates FOMO and urgency — when competitors gain advantage through AI, companies must respond to protect market position

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which of your competitors have impressed you with their AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 Are you losing ground to AI-native competitors?
  • 🔍 What competitive advantage are you trying to build with AI?
