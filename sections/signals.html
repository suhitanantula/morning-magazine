Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically for AI - indicates formalised AI strategy and executive-level commitment to transformation

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive appointment press

Why powerful: Creates a direct decision-maker with AI mandate; signals company is moving beyond experimentation to enterprise-wide transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did you create the CAIO role and what mandate were they given?
  • 🔍 How is the CAIO structured to report and what budget do they control?
  • 🔍 What was the board's reasoning for creating this role now?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO or board explicitly stated AI as strategic priority in public communications

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, annual report CEO letter

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability and force resource allocation; demonstrates board and executive alignment on AI as competitive imperative

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What specific AI outcomes was the CEO committing to in that mandate?
  • 🔍 How has that public mandate translated into internal resource allocation?
  • 🔍 What mechanisms are in place to track progress against that AI-first commitment?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure on AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy visible in board materials, AGM discussions, or director communications

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussion minutes, board committee creation

Why powerful: Board pressure is the most reliable forcing function for executive action - it creates urgency, justifies budget, and overcomes internal resistance to transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What specific AI topics has the board been discussing recently?
  • 🔍 Are there board directors with AI expertise driving these conversations?
  • 🔍 What board-level governance structures have been created for AI oversight?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with track record of AI transformation or explicit AI priority in their mandate

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO LinkedIn profile

Why powerful: New CEOs have board backing, no legacy systems to protect, and authority to restructure for transformation - they are natural change agents

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What AI transformation experience does the incoming CEO bring?
  • 🔍 What was the board's explicit mandate to the new CEO regarding AI?
  • 🔍 How has the new CEO signalled AI priority in their first 100 days?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team building

What it is: 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure rather than buying SaaS solutions

Examples: Job postings for ML engineers, engineering blog posts, LinkedIn hiring for RAG/agents/embeddings, internal hackathon announcements

Why powerful: Building internal capability signals serious, long-term transformation commitment - these companies are investing in AI as core competency, not one-off projects

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What internal AI platform is your team building and what's the architectural approach?
  • 🔍 How many engineers are dedicated to AI infrastructure vs AI applications?
  • 🔍 What's the timeline for moving from platform build to production scale?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: 5+ AI-specific hires in 6 months including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, recruitment announcements, talent competitor departures

Why powerful: Hiring at scale indicates strategic intent and budget approval for AI capability building - validates the transformation is real and resourced

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving the recent AI hiring surge - new projects or headcount expansion?
  • 🔍 Which AI roles have been hardest to fill and what compensation trends are you seeing?
  • 🔍 How are you integrating these new AI hires into existing teams?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Elimination of old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog posts, case studies, press mentions of workflow transformation, internal process documentation

Why powerful: Process change proves AI is moving beyond pilots into operational transformation - companies are redesigning work, not just adding AI tools

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which core processes have you redesigned around AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 What旧的工作流程已被AI消除或自动化?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring productivity gains from process redesign?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators rather than just buying SaaS tools

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, system integrator engagements, AI implementation partnerships

Why powerful: Engaging integrators signals complexity and scale - these companies need external expertise for transformation they cannot do alone

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which AI partners or integrators are you working with?
  • 🔍 What specific capabilities are you building vs buying through partners?
  • 🔍 How do you evaluate success of these AI partnerships?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: 50%+ year-over-year increase in AI budget

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, CFO statements, internal budget approvals

Why powerful: Budget increases prove commitment at the resource level - it's easy to talk about AI, but budget allocation is the ultimate signal of seriousness

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving the year-over-year AI budget increase?
  • 🔍 How is the AI budget allocated between CapEx and OpEx?
  • 🔍 What's the expected ROI framework for this increased AI investment?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements that affect the company's sector

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Government action creates urgency, forces companies to respond, and often opens funding or compliance requirements that accelerate AI adoption

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is your sector responding to recent government AI policy announcements?
  • 🔍 What compliance or regulatory requirements are driving your AI timeline?
  • 🔍 Are you accessing any government AI funding or incentive programs?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: Company's sector undergoing AI-driven disruption from new entrants, margin compression, or customer behaviour shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, industry analyst coverage, market research

Why powerful: External disruption creates urgency and justifies transformation investment - companies in disrupted sectors have no choice but to act

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is AI changing the competitive dynamics in your sector?
  • 🔍 What market share are new AI-native entrants gaining?
  • 🔍 How are customer expectations shifting due to AI-enabled alternatives?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, competitive intelligence

Why powerful: Competitor action creates FOMO and validates that AI transformation provides real competitive advantage - helps justify investment internally

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which competitors have made significant AI announcements?
  • 🔍 What's your competitive intelligence on competitor AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 How are you differentiating your AI strategy from competitors?
