Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically for AI indicates formalised AI transformation mandate and budget authority

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive team org chart

Why powerful: Creates a budget-wielding champion with executive authority to drive transformation. Signals org restructure is already underway.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did you create your AI leadership role and what mandate did they receive?
  • 🔍 How is your CAIO positioned in the org structure — direct to CEO or board?
  • 🔍 What's the headcount and budget your AI leadership has been given?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO or board explicitly stated AI as strategic priority in public communications

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, annual report CEO letter

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability and resource allocation pressure. Cannot be walked back without reputational cost.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Can you share what the CEO's public position on AI transformation is?
  • 🔍 What specific outcomes has leadership committed to for AI in the next 12-18 months?
  • 🔍 How is the AI mandate being communicated internally across the organisation?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure on AI strategy

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy, AI initiatives appearing in board materials

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussions, board committee creation

Why powerful: Board pressure forces executive action. Directors are hearing from investors about AI and will push management to demonstrate progress.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How frequently is AI appearing on your board agenda currently?
  • 🔍 What specific board-level AI initiatives or commitments have been made?
  • 🔍 Are your directors receiving AI education or briefing sessions?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with track record of AI transformation or explicit AI priority signal

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, inaugural CEO speech

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and no legacy systems to protect. They have permission to make bold moves.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the new CEO's background with AI transformation?
  • 🔍 What has the CEO specifically said about AI as a priority?
  • 🔍 Has the board given the CEO specific AI transformation objectives?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team building

What it is: 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure (RAG, agents, embeddings pipelines) rather than buying SaaS

Examples: Job postings for ML engineers, engineering blog posts, LinkedIn engineering team growth, internal tech talks

Why powerful: Building internal capability signals serious investment and long-term commitment. These teams become internal champions for expansion.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the size and structure of your internal AI engineering team?
  • 🔍 What internal AI platforms or infrastructure are you building?
  • 🔍 Are you building to replace vendor solutions or creating net-new capabilities?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: 5+ AI-specific hires in 6 months including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, recruitment announcements

Why powerful: Hiring velocity indicates budget approval and urgency. Shows organisation is scaling beyond experimentation.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How many AI-focused hires have you made in the last 6 months?
  • 🔍 What roles have been most critical to your AI strategy?
  • 🔍 What's your hiring pipeline look like for the next quarter?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Elimination of old workflows, creation of new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog case studies, press mentions, internal process documentation, operational metrics

Why powerful: Real transformation touches process, not just technology. Process change demonstrates proof of value and scale.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What existing processes have you redesigned or replaced with AI?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring the impact of AI on operational workflows?
  • 🔍 What's the biggest process transformation you've achieved with AI so far?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators rather than just buying SaaS tools

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, SI engagement announcements

Why powerful: Engaging integrators shows willingness to invest in transformation, not just tools. Often leads to larger strategic engagements.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which AI consulting or integration partners are you working with?
  • 🔍 What specific transformation work are you doing with them?
  • 🔍 What's the scope and duration of your current AI partnerships?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: 50%+ Year-over-Year increase in AI budget

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, CFO communications, board budget approvals

Why powerful: Budget trajectory indicates strategic commitment. 50%+ growth suggests AI moving from pilot to production scale.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How has your AI budget changed year-over-year?
  • 🔍 What's driving the increase — headcount, infrastructure, or partner costs?
  • 🔍 What's your AI budget as a percentage of overall technology spend?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements affecting company's sector

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Creates urgency, forces companies to respond, opens funding opportunities. Regulatory compliance becomes a catalyst for transformation.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is government policy or regulation affecting your AI strategy?
  • 🔍 Are there compliance deadlines driving your AI investment timeline?
  • 🔍 Has government funding or incentives influenced your AI decisions?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: Company's sector undergoing AI-driven disruption from new entrants, margin compression, customer behaviour shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, industry analyst coverage, market research

Why powerful: External disruption creates strategic urgency. Companies must respond to survive, making them more receptive to BD conversations.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How do you see AI disrupting your industry over the next 3-5 years?
  • 🔍 What competitive threats are you seeing from AI-native entrants?
  • 🔍 How is customer behaviour changing in your sector due to AI?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, analyst comparisons

Why powerful: Competitive pressure is a powerful catalyst. No one wants to be left behind, especially to a competitor.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which competitors are you watching most closely on AI?
  • 🔍 What AI moves have competitors made that concern you?
  • 🔍 How are you differentiating from competitors on AI capability?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

New AI features announced

What it is: Product updates adding AI capabilities but not representing org-wide transformation

Examples: Product launch announcements, feature release notes, app updates

Why powerful: Indicates awareness and experimentation but not necessarily strategic commitment. Useful context but not primary qualifier.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the extent of your AI product roadmap?
  • 🔍 Are these features built or bought?
  • 🔍 How are customers responding to the AI features?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI training programs

What it is: Internal upskilling initiatives without evidence of process change

Examples: Training announcements, learning platform launches, internal L&D communications

Why powerful: Shows investment in workforce but may not indicate strategic transformation. Useful for culture but not qualification.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the scale of your AI upskilling program?
  • 🔍 How does training connect to actual AI implementation?
  • 🔍 Are you training for adoption or for building AI capability?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI task force / committee

What it is: Planning bodies without evidence of investment or execution

Examples: Internal announcements, committee charters, working group formations

Why powerful: Often indicates early-stage consideration but not commitment. Many task forces never progress beyond planning.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What has the task force achieved so far?
  • 🔍 What's the budget and authority of the committee?
  • 🔍 What's the timeline from planning to execution?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Single AI hire

What it is: Addition of one AI role without structural change

Examples: Single job posting, one LinkedIn hire announcement

Why powerful: Low signal strength. Could be a single experiment or box-ticking exercise. Needs strong signals to qualify.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Is the single hire part of a broader hiring strategy?
  • 🔍 What mandate does this role have?
  • 🔍 How is this role connected to business outcomes?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI mentioned in annual report

What it is: Compliance or box-ticking mention without strategic substance

Examples: Annual report text, ESG section, risk disclosure

Why powerful: Lowest signal strength. Nearly every company mentions AI now. Must be combined with other signals to matter.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Beyond the annual report mention, what's the actual AI strategy?
  • 🔍 How much investment is behind the AI direction in the report?
  • 🔍 Who owns execution of the AI strategy?
