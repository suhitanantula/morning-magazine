Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically for AI

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing

Why powerful: C-suite commitment signals board-level priority and dedicated budget for AI transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What triggered the decision to create a dedicated AI leadership role?
  • 🔍 How is the CAIO structured to drive cross-functional AI adoption?
  • 🔍 What mandate and budget has the CAIO been given?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO or board explicitly said AI-first or AI transformation in public communications

Examples: Earnings call, investor day, press interview, annual report strategy section

Why powerful: Public commitments create investor expectations and internal pressure to deliver, making transformation non-optional

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What does your AI-first strategy mean for the next 18 months?
  • 🔍 How is executive compensation tied to AI transformation goals?
  • 🔍 What resources have been allocated to deliver on this public mandate?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure / Board directors talking about AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy, AI initiatives in board materials

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussions, board committee creation

Why powerful: Board pressure forces executive action and budget allocation - directors are asking tough questions about AI readiness

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What AI questions are your board members asking most frequently?
  • 🔍 Has the board established an AI steering committee or dedicated oversight?
  • 🔍 What board-level metrics are tracking AI transformation progress?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: New CEO within 12-18 months with track record of AI transformation or AI as priority

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO prior AI work, mandate letters, board briefing documents

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and no legacy systems to protect - they can make bold AI decisions

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What is your vision for AI-driven transformation in your first term?
  • 🔍 How does your board expect AI to change the competitive position?
  • 🔍 What legacy systems or processes are you evaluating for AI-native replacement?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team building not buying

What it is: 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure - RAG systems, agents, embeddings pipelines

Examples: Job postings, engineering blog, GitHub activity, LinkedIn technical posts

Why powerful: Building internal capability signals serious investment and desire for proprietary AI differentiation, not just point solutions

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What internal AI infrastructure is your team building?
  • 🔍 How are you approaching the build-vs-buy decision for AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 What engineering resources are dedicated to AI platform development?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: 5+ AI-specific hires in 6 months - prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, talent acquisition announcements

Why powerful: Hiring at scale indicates structural commitment and resource allocation, not just pilot projects

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What AI roles are you prioritizing in your hiring roadmap?
  • 🔍 How are you competing for AI talent against larger tech companies?
  • 🔍 What existing team members are being upskilled alongside new hires?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Eliminated old workflows, created new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog, case study, press mention, internal documentation

Why powerful: Real transformation involves process change, not just technology adoption - this shows deep implementation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which legacy processes are being reimagined with AI?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring efficiency gains from process redesign?
  • 🔍 What role do employees play in identifying AI-native process opportunities?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators, not just buying SaaS tools

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, SI engagements

Why powerful: Working with integrators suggests complex transformation requiring external expertise, not simple tool implementation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What external AI partners are you working with currently?
  • 🔍 How are you structuring the consultant-to-internal-knowledge transfer?
  • 🔍 What capability gaps are you bridging through partnerships?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: 50%+ Year-over-Year increase in AI budget

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, CFO statements

Why powerful: Budget increases reflect executive commitment and enable meaningful transformation scope, not just experiments

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What is driving your AI budget increase this year?
  • 🔍 How is AI budget allocated between experimentation and production workloads?
  • 🔍 What ROI expectations are attached to the increased AI investment?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements affecting the companys sector

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, funding programs

Why powerful: Creates urgency, forces companies to respond, opens funding opportunities, and can reshape competitive dynamics

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is regulatory change in your sector driving AI adoption priorities?
  • 🔍 Are you accessing any government AI incentive or funding programs?
  • 🔍 How are you preparing for sector-specific AI compliance requirements?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: Sector undergoing AI-driven disruption - new entrants, margin compression, customer behavior shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, increasing industry analyst coverage

Why powerful: External disruption creates strategic urgency and makes transformation a survival imperative, not a choice

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How do you see AI changing the competitive dynamics in your sector?
  • 🔍 What new market entrants are forcing a response on AI capability?
  • 🔍 How are customer expectations shifting due to AI-enabled alternatives?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives, gaining market share through AI

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, analyst coverage

Why powerful: Competitor success with AI creates proof of concept and raises stakes - being left behind becomes a visible risk

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which competitors are you watching most closely on AI capability?
  • 🔍 What has surprised you about competitor AI implementations?
  • 🔍 How are you positioning to differentiate from AI-enabled competitors?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

New AI features announced

What it is: Product updates or feature releases incorporating AI

Examples: Product announcements, release notes, marketing materials

Why powerful: Product-level AI shows innovation intent but does not necessarily indicate organizational transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How are AI features changing your product roadmap priorities?
  • 🔍 What customer feedback is driving AI feature investment?
  • 🔍 Are AI capabilities becoming a primary competitive differentiator?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI training programs

What it is: Internal upskilling without process change

Examples: Training announcements, learning platform investments, certification programs

Why powerful: Upskilling is necessary but insufficient - without process change, training alone does not drive transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is AI training translating into actual workflow changes?
  • 🔍 What percentage of staff has completed AI upskilling?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring training impact on productivity?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI task force / committee

What it is: Planning without investment - governance structure but no budget

Examples: Internal announcements, steering committee formation, strategy documents

Why powerful: Task forces can signal intent but often indicate early-stage exploration rather than committed transformation

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What has the task force accomplished to date?
  • 🔍 What budget and authority does the task force have?
  • 🔍 How is the task force engagement evolving into execution?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Single AI hire

What it is: Added one role without structural change

Examples: Individual hire announcement, single role creation

Why powerful: Single hires may be tactical experiments rather than strategic transformation signals

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What is the mandate for this AI role?
  • 🔍 How is this role connected to broader AI strategy?
  • 🔍 What resources support this individual?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI mentioned in annual report

What it is: Compliance box-ticking - AI referenced without substance

Examples: Annual report text, sustainability section, risk disclosure

Why powerful: Mention without commitment indicates awareness but not action - useful context but not a strong signal

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What specific AI initiatives are tied to the mentions in your report?
  • 🔍 How is AI referenced in your risk and opportunity framework?
  • 🔍 What investor questions are you receiving about AI strategy?
