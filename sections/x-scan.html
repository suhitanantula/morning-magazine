80% of Enterprise AI Projects Fail to Deliver Business Value

RAND Corporation research reveals 80.3% of AI initiatives fail to deliver intended business value, with 33.8% abandoned before production and 42% of companies abandoning at least one AI initiative in 2025.

This follows MIT findings that 95% of pilot projects deliver zero financial return due to vanity use cases, treating AI as IT projects rather than business decisions, building on broken data, and lacking production readiness planning.

84% of AI Project Failures Trace to Leadership Issues, Not Technical Problems

Research shows 73% of failed AI projects lack clear executive alignment on success metrics, 68% underinvest in data foundations, and 56% lose active C-suite sponsorship within six months.

Projects with sustained CEO involvement achieve 68% success rates versus 11% for those losing sponsorship.

Organizations also struggle with governance: 54% admit adopting AI too quickly and now face challenges scaling back responsibly.

GOVERNMENT

Australian AI Safety Institute Launches with Lighter-Touch Regulatory Approach

Australia launched AISI in November 2025 with $29.9M funding, now operational early 2026 as advisory body for AI safety evaluation focusing on pre-deployment testing of advanced AI systems.

Government moved away from mandatory guardrails approach, adopting lighter-touch regulatory strategy focused on safety evaluation rather than prescriptive rules.

National AI Plan released with three-pronged approach focusing on infrastructure, skills development, and safety through existing legal frameworks.
