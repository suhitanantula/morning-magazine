Tech Industry Layoffs: AI-Washing Masks Workforce Restructuring

Tech industry cut 78,557 jobs in Q1 2026 with 47.9% attributed to AI—nearly 37,638 positions.

But Goldman Sachs estimates genuine AI-driven job losses at only 5,000-10,000 monthly across all US sectors.

Oracle led with ~25,000 cuts while raising $50B for AI data centers; Amazon cut ~16,000, Microsoft ~15,347.

The gap between executive rhetoric and economic reality reveals widespread 'AI-washing'—cost cuts dressed in AI justification.

Meanwhile AI job postings surged 92% with 56% wage premiums for ML engineers and prompt engineers.

GOVERNMENT

Australia Shifts to Lighter-Touch AI Regulation

Australia dropped planned mandatory AI guardrails in favor of an AI Safety Institute (AUD $29.9M funding) with focus on testing and monitoring rather than prescriptive rules.

The National AI Plan released three-pronged approach: infrastructure, skills development, and safety through existing legal frameworks.

Public sector agencies now required to have chief AI officers.

Government scrapped an AI Advisory Body after spending AUD $188,337 following Productivity Commission advice to pause regulatory work.

Q1 2026 AI Investment Hits Record $300B Globally
