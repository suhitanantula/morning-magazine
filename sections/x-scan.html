MARKET

Q1 2026 AI Funding Explosions: $300B Global Investment, 80% Into AI

Global venture investment shattered records with $300 billion into 6,000 startups in Q1 2026, up 150%+ QoQ and YoY.

AI captured $242B (80% of total).

The five largest rounds—OpenAI $122B, Anthropic $30B, xAI $20B, Waymo $16B—represent 65% of all global venture investment in a single quarter.

US captured $250B (83% of global), up from 71% a year prior.

Seed deal counts fell 30% YoY even as dollar amounts rose—capital is concentrating into later-stage rounds rather than dispersing into early-stage experimentation.

GOVERNMENT

Australia's National AI Plan: $1B+ Committed, Safety Institute Funded

The Australian government formally tabled its response to the Senate AI Committee report, anchoring policy to the National AI Plan (December 2025).

Three goals: capture the opportunity, spread benefits, keep Australians safe.

Key commitments: $29.8M for AI Safety Institute, $1B through National Reconstruction Fund for critical technologies, $5M GenAI pilots in schools.

The government scrapped a planned AI Advisory Body after spending AUD $188,337—opting for a lighter-touch regulatory approach centered on the Safety Institute.

GOVERNANCE

Gartner: 40% of AI Agent Projects to Be Cancelled by 2027—Governance Failure

Gartner forecasts 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027, not because of technology failure but governance failure.

53% of marketing organisations using AI agents have no governance framework in place.

The three primary failure drivers: undefined uncertainty handling, unstated acceptable error rates, and diffuse accountability without monitoring.

Separately, only 28% of AI infrastructure projects deliver ROI, with 20% failing outright—and 77% of failures are organizational, not technical.

Enterprise Software Crisis: 20-40% Stock Drops, Oracle Cutting 30,000

Six of the largest enterprise software vendors lost between 20% and 40% of market value in early 2026.

ASX Tech Sector Collapse: 44% Decline Since September 2025

The S&P/ASX 200 Information Technology Index has fallen 44% since September 2025, driven by AI disruption fears threatening SaaS business models.

WiseTech Global fell 10% in a single day to $39; Xero dropped 8.28% to $73.64; Zip lost 9.40% to $1.81.

The ASX 200 fell 940 points (10.20%) from its 9202.9 record high.

Fund managers are gravitating toward 'HALO' trades (Heavy Assets, Low Obsolescence) and backing defensible businesses like Wisetech (network effects) and Megaport (picks and shovels).

Small-cap ASX software trading at median 1.6x versus historical 3-5x—creating M&A arbitrage opportunity.
