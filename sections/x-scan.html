MARKET

Q1 2026 AI Funding Hits $300B as Capital Concentrates in Five Players

Global venture investment shattered records with $300 billion into 6,000 startups, up 150%+ QoQ and YoY.

AI captured $242B (80% of all funding).

The five largest rounds—OpenAI $122B, Anthropic $30B, xAI $20B, Waymo $16B—represent 65% of global venture investment in a single quarter.

US captured $250B (83% of global), up from 71% a year prior.

Seed deal counts fell 30% YoY as capital concentrates into later-stage rounds rather than dispersing into early-stage experimentation.

GOVERNMENT

Australia Tabs National AI Plan with $1B Government Commitment

The federal government formally responded to the Senate AI Committee report, anchoring policy to the National AI Plan with three goals: capture the opportunity, spread benefits, keep Australians safe.

Key commitments include $29.8M for AI Safety Institute, $1B through National Reconstruction Fund for critical technologies, and $5M GenAI pilots in schools.

The government scrapped a planned AI Advisory Body after spending AUD $188,337, pivoting to a lighter-touch regulatory approach.

🔗 Dig deeper · Department of Industry
💡 Why it matters: Australia's $1B commitment signals government-backed AI infrastructure projects coming to market. The lighter-touch regulatory approach means faster procurement cycles—local firms with governance capabilities have a narrow window to establish position before competitors flood the market.
MARKET

161,000+ Employees Impacted by AI-Linked Corporate Restructuring

Across 45+ companies in 2025-2026, enterprises including Microsoft (15,000), Amazon (30,000), Intel (24,000), and Oracle (30,000) have explicitly cited AI efficiency as the driver for layoffs.

Q1 2026 saw 78,557 tech workers lose jobs, with 47.9% (37,638 positions) directly linked to AI implementation.

Oracle alone cut 25,254 roles while simultaneously raising $50B for AI data centre expansion.

However, Goldman Sachs estimates only 5,000-10,000 genuine AI job losses monthly—suggesting widespread 'AI-washing' of traditional cost cuts.

Gartner: 40% of Agentic AI Projects to Be Cancelled by 2027

Gartner forecasts 40% of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by 2027, not because of technology failure but governance failure.

53% of marketing organisations using AI agents have no governance framework in place.

The three failure drivers: undefined uncertainty handling, unstated acceptable error rates, and diffuse accountability without monitoring.

This follows Gartner data showing only 28% of AI infrastructure projects deliver ROI with 20% failing outright.

🔗 Dig deeper · Matheus Vizotto
💡 Why it matters: The governance failure rate (40% cancelled, 77% organizational failure) is the market gap Helix Lab fills. The problem isn't 'we need better models'—it's 'we need measurement frameworks, business ownership, and governance design before deployment.' This is exactly where advisory services create value.

73% of Enterprises Fail to Achieve Intended Benefits from First AI Implementation

60% of AI projects never progress beyond pilot phase.

Primary causes: lack of executive sponsorship, insufficient data governance, unclear accountability, inadequate risk assessment, and missing continuous monitoring frameworks.

Only 15% of companies report having mature AI governance frameworks.

Among infrastructure leaders who faced AI setbacks, 38% cited persistent skill gaps and another 38% identified poor data quality as direct failure causes.

🔗 Dig deeper · ITPI
💡 Why it matters: The 73% failure rate and 15% mature governance adoption create a massive services TAM. COMPETITIVE

Enterprise Software Crisis: 20-40% Stock Drops as AI Monetisation Fails

Six largest enterprise software vendors lost 20-40% of market value in early 2026.

Oracle cut up to 30,000 roles while raising $50B for AI data centres—down 25% YTD.

Microsoft down ~23%, Adobe ~31%, Salesforce ~24%, ServiceNow ~40%.

Hyperscalers committed $350B+ in AI capex in 2025 but cannot demonstrate returns.

When vendors face heavy capital commitments and disappointing revenue growth, revenue recovery moves from new sales to existing customer base through compliance enforcement—Broadcom transformed VMware licensing with some customers reporting 1,000%+ cost increases.

💡 Why it matters: Enterprise software crisis creates two opportunities for Helix Lab: (1) Compliance enforcement pain is coming—clients need help managing vendor audits and licensing defensibly, (2) The AI monetisation gap is the market gap—vendors cannot show AI returns, creating demand for help demonstrating ROI.

ASX Tech Sector Down 44% Since September as AI Disruption Fears Mount

The S&P/ASX 200 Information Technology Index has fallen 44% since September 2025 as AI fears emerge as the primary driver.

This is structural repricing—AI is threatening SaaS business models.

Four of six biggest ASX 200 companies by market cap are SaaS providers.

Fund managers are backing businesses with stronger barriers: Wisetech (network effects, switching costs) and Megaport (picks and shovels) are being accumulated at highly discounted valuations.

Helix Lab should position as the advisor helping companies build structural defensibility against AI disruption.
