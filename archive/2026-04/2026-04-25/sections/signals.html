Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: A new C-suite role specifically created for AI leadership indicates formal organizational commitment to AI transformation at the highest level.

Examples: LinkedIn announcement from CEO, press release, board filing, executive appointment press

Why powerful: Creates dedicated senior executive accountability for AI outcomes, signals board-level commitment, and typically comes with budget and hiring authority.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When was your Chief AI Officer or VP of AI appointed, and what's their mandate?
  • 🔍 What reporting structure does your AI leader have - do they report to CEO or CTO?
  • 🔍 What's the budget and headcount under your AI leadership role?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO, board, or key executives have explicitly stated AI as a strategic priority in public communications such as earnings calls, investor days, or press interviews.

Examples: Earnings call transcript mentioning 'AI-first', investor day presentation, CEO interview in trade publication, annual report letter

Why powerful: Public commitments create organizational pressure to deliver, investor expectations, and indicate resource allocation will follow the strategy.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Can you share what 'AI-first' means for your organization specifically?
  • 🔍 What did the CEO commit to in terms of AI timeline or outcomes?
  • 🔍 How is this mandate being communicated internally to the broader organization?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure / Board directors talking about AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy, AI initiatives appearing in board materials, or board directors publicly discussing AI priorities.

Examples: Board announcement on AI strategy, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM discussion of AI, board committee creation for AI

Why powerful: Board pressure forces executive action and creates urgency. Board-mandated initiatives typically receive funding and executive attention that organic projects don't.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How frequently is AI appearing on your board agenda currently?
  • 🔍 Has the board established any specific AI governance or oversight committee?
  • 🔍 What board-level directives have been communicated to management on AI?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: A new CEO appointed within 12-18 months who has a track record of AI transformation or has explicitly signaled AI as a priority in their mandate.

Examples: CEO announcement with AI background, incoming CEO's prior AI work at previous company, mandate letter mentioning AI transformation

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and don't have legacy systems or relationships to protect. They're most likely to make bold AI investments.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did your current CEO join, and what was their AI background?
  • 🔍 What has the CEO said about AI in their first 100 days or public communications?
  • 🔍 Has the CEO made any structural changes that indicate AI priority?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team building not buying

What it is: Evidence of 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure including RAG systems, agents, embeddings pipelines, or custom AI platforms.

Examples: Job postings for ML engineers, engineering blog posts about AI infrastructure, LinkedIn profiles of growing AI engineering team

Why powerful: Building internal platforms indicates significant investment and long-term commitment. These companies are solving complex problems and need strategic partners, not just point solutions.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the size of your internal AI engineering team currently?
  • 🔍 What internal AI infrastructure or platforms are you building?
  • 🔍 Are you building vs buying for your core AI capabilities?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: A significant surge in AI-specific hires including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers, or data scientists within a 6-month period.

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals showing 5+ AI roles, job posting surge on careers page, recruitment announcements

Why powerful: Hiring sprees indicate scaling AI initiatives and creating internal capability. The scale of hiring signals the size of ambition.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How many AI-focused hires have you made in the last 6-12 months?
  • 🔍 What's driving your AI hiring push - new initiatives or scaling existing work?
  • 🔍 What roles have been most difficult to fill?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Evidence of eliminated old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes, indicating transformation rather than incremental improvement.

Examples: Company blog about process transformation, case study showing workflow elimination, press mention of new AI-driven processes

Why powerful: Process redesign indicates true transformation mindset, not just adding AI features to existing workflows. These companies are reimagining how work gets done.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What legacy processes have you eliminated or redesigned with AI?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring the impact of these process changes?
  • 🔍 What's the biggest workflow transformation you've achieved with AI so far?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies, system integrators, or strategic AI partners rather than just buying SaaS tools.

Examples: Partner announcements with consulting firms, case studies showing consultancy engagement, system integrator project announcements

Why powerful: Engaging integrators indicates complex transformation requiring external expertise. These companies acknowledge they need help and are investing in strategic relationships.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which AI consultancies or integrators are you working with currently?
  • 🔍 What type of AI transformation work are you partnering on?
  • 🔍 How do you decide what to build internally vs partner on?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: A material increase in AI budget year-over-year, typically 50%+ increase indicating scaling commitment.

Examples: Budget filings showing AI allocation, investor presentations with AI investment details, CFO comments on AI spending

Why powerful: Budget increases directly enable AI projects and hiring. 50%+ increases indicate strategic scaling, not pilot experimentation.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's your current AI budget allocation and how has it changed year-over-year?
  • 🔍 How is your AI budget split between different initiatives?
  • 🔍 What percentage of overall tech budget goes to AI?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians, bureaucrats, or regulators making AI announcements that affect the company's sector, creating urgency and opening funding opportunities.

Examples: Government AI strategy announcements, regulatory changes affecting sector, policy consultations, public sector AI funding programs

Why powerful: Policy shifts create urgency, force companies to respond, and often open government funding or support programs. They can accelerate otherwise slow decision-making.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is recent government AI policy affecting your sector?
  • 🔍 Are you pursuing any government AI funding or support programs?
  • 🔍 What regulatory changes are you preparing for in AI?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: The company's sector is undergoing AI-driven disruption through new entrants, margin compression, or customer behavior shifts driven by AI.

Examples: Analyst reports on AI disruption in sector, trade publication coverage, increasing industry analyst attention, market share shifts

Why powerful: Disruption creates urgency and justifies transformation investment. Companies in disrupted industries are more likely to be receptive to AI partnerships.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How do you see AI disrupting your industry over the next 2-3 years?
  • 🔍 What new AI-driven competitors or entrants have you observed?
  • 🔍 How is AI changing customer expectations in your sector?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing significant AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI-driven advantages.

Examples: Competitor press releases on AI, industry news on competitor AI projects, market share data showing AI-advantaged competitors

Why powerful: Competitor activity creates competitive pressure and FOMO. Companies are more likely to invest when they see peers succeeding with AI.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which competitors do you see leading on AI in your space?
  • 🔍 What competitor AI initiatives are you monitoring?
  • 🔍 How are you thinking about competitive differentiation through AI?
