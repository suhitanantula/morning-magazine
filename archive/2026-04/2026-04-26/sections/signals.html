Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically for AI indicates strategic commitment and organizational structure change

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive team org chart update

Why powerful: Creation of a C-suite role dedicated to AI signals board-level commitment and dedicated budget. This role exists only when AI transformation is a strategic priority, not a tactical experiment.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Can you tell me about your current AI leadership structure? Do you have a CAIO or VP of AI?
  • 🔍 How is your AI function organized at the executive level?
  • 🔍 What role does AI play in your current executive team structure?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO or board explicitly stated AI as strategic priority in public communications

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, annual report letter from CEO

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability. When CEOs say 'AI-first' to investors, they must deliver. This creates internal pressure and budget allocation that BD opportunities can capitalize on.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 I saw the CEO's comments about AI transformation at [event]. How is that translating into execution?
  • 🔍 What's the current priority for AI in [company] given the public mandate?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring progress against the AI-first commitment announced earlier this year?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure / Board directors talking about AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy appearing in board materials, announcements, or director communications

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussions, board committee creation

Why powerful: Board pressure forces executive action. Directors who ask about AI at board meetings create urgency that bypasses typical corporate inertia. This pressure flows down to procurement and strategy decisions.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is your board driving the AI transformation agenda?
  • 🔍 What board-level conversations are happening about AI strategy?
  • 🔍 Are you feeling pressure from the board to show AI progress?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with track record of AI transformation or who has signaled AI as priority

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO interview about AI vision

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and no legacy systems to protect. They are looking for quick wins to establish credibility and AI is a high-visibility priority.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is the new leadership team approaching AI transformation?
  • 🔍 What's the mandate from the new CEO on AI?
  • 🔍 As new leadership settles in, what are the priority initiatives for the next 12 months?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team (building not buying)

What it is: 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure including RAG, agents, embeddings pipelines

Examples: Engineering blog posts, job postings for platform engineers, GitHub activity, tech conference talks

Why powerful: Companies building internal platforms have serious investment and technical ambition. They are moving beyond point solutions to foundational capabilities—a sign of transformation, not experimentation.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's your current AI platform architecture looking like?
  • 🔍 Are you building internally or leveraging external partners for your AI infrastructure?
  • 🔍 How big is your internal AI engineering team?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: 5+ AI-specific hires in 6 months including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, recruitment announcements, talent competition wins

Why powerful: Hiring at scale indicates commitment. Multiple hires suggest a program, not a project. However, hiring alone doesn't guarantee budget or strategy alignment—it adds context.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How has your AI team grown recently?
  • 🔍 What roles are you prioritizing in your AI hiring?
  • 🔍 What's the current headcount in your AI/ML function?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Eliminated old workflows and created new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog case studies, press mentions, internal process documentation, operational efficiency announcements

Why powerful: Process change is the hallmark of transformation vs. experimentation. When companies redesign workflows around AI, they are committed to measurable business impact, not just technology pilots.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What processes are you reimagining with AI?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring the impact of AI on your operations?
  • 🔍 What workflows have been transformed so far?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators rather than just buying SaaS tools

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, system integrator engagements, AI boutique partnerships

Why powerful: Companies working with integrators are serious about transformation. They recognize they need external expertise. This signals openness to partnership and budget for professional services.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Who are you working with to execute your AI strategy?
  • 🔍 Are you partnering with external consultants for your AI transformation?
  • 🔍 What does your partner ecosystem look like for AI implementation?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: 50%+ year-over-year increase in AI budget

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, CFO communications, departmental budget approvals

Why powerful: Budget increases indicate organizational commitment. A 50%+ YOY increase suggests AI is moving from experimental to core. However, budget doesn't always equal execution capability.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How has your AI budget evolved this year?
  • 🔍 What's the current investment level in AI transformation?
  • 🔍 Are you seeing adequate budget allocation for AI initiatives?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements that affect the company's sector

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Government action creates urgency and forces companies to respond. Policy shifts often open funding opportunities and compliance requirements that drive procurement decisions.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How are you responding to the regulatory environment around AI in your sector?
  • 🔍 What impact do recent government AI policies have on your strategy?
  • 🔍 Are there compliance requirements driving your AI investment?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: Company's sector undergoing AI-driven disruption through new entrants, margin compression, customer behavior shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, industry analyst coverage, market research

Why powerful: Disruption creates urgency. When an industry is being transformed by AI, companies must respond or risk irrelevance. This context helps prioritize which prospects are most motivated.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is AI changing the competitive dynamics in your industry?
  • 🔍 What disruption are you seeing from AI in your sector?
  • 🔍 How are your competitors using AI to gain advantage?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, analyst comparisons

Why powerful: Competitor activity creates FOMO. When rivals announce AI wins, prospects feel pressure to respond. This fear drives BD conversations and accelerates procurement timelines.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What are your competitors doing with AI?
  • 🔍 How are you positioning against competitors who are investing heavily in AI?
  • 🔍 What AI moves have you seen from key players in your market?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

New AI features announced

What it is: Product updates or feature releases incorporating AI without organizational transformation

Examples: Product launch announcements, feature blog posts, app updates

Why powerful: Feature releases are tactical product decisions, not strategic transformation. Low BD priority as these don't indicate organizational readiness for comprehensive AI change.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Beyond product features, how are you thinking about AI at an organizational level?
  • 🔍 Is AI driving product strategy or is it a feature add-on?
  • 🔍 What's the broader AI vision beyond product updates?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

AI training programs

What it is: Internal upskilling initiatives without evidence of process change or investment

Examples: L&D announcements, training program launches, skill certification programs

Why powerful: Training alone is insufficient—it often signals performative activity without actual transformation intent. Indicates awareness but not commitment to change.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How are you moving from training to actual AI implementation?
  • 🔍 What's the connection between your training programs and business transformation?
  • 🔍 Are you seeing behavioral change from your AI training initiatives?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

AI task force / committee

What it is: Planning bodies or committees without evidence of investment or execution

Examples: Committee formation announcements, working groups, steering committees

Why powerful: Task forces are often political theater—signals planning but not action. Many companies form committees to appear responsive without committing resources.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the output of your AI task force so far?
  • 🔍 How is the committee translating into execution?
  • 🔍 What resources are supporting the task force's work?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

Single AI hire

What it is: Addition of one AI role without structural change or additional investment

Examples: One job posting, single hire announcement

Why powerful: Single hires are often checkbox exercises. One role without team, budget, or mandate rarely indicates transformation readiness.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Is the single hire part of a broader team build?
  • 🔍 What's the mandate for this role?
  • 🔍 How is success defined for this position?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

AI mentioned in annual report

What it is: Compliance box-ticking with AI language in regulatory filings without substance

Examples: Annual report paragraphs, ESG sections, risk disclosures

Why powerful: Mandatory disclosure language is low-signal. Every company mentions AI now—this is baseline, not a differentiator. Indicates awareness but not commitment.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Beyond the annual report mention, what's the actual AI strategy?
  • 🔍 How is the board-level discussion on AI reflected in the report?
  • 🔍 What's the gap between the report and execution?
