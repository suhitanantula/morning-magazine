Australia Launches AI Safety Institute Under Lighter-Touch Regulatory Framework

Australia launched the AI Safety Institute in November 2025 with $29.9M funding, now operational as an advisory body for AI safety evaluation focusing on pre-deployment testing rather than regulatory enforcement.

Government moved away from mandatory guardrails, adopting lighter-touch strategy that complements existing regulators (OAIC, ACCC, eSafety) while identifying gaps where current laws fail to address AI-specific risks.

The National AI Plan commits $1B through National Reconstruction Fund for critical technologies.
