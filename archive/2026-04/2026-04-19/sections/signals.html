Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer Created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically created for AI leadership, signaling board-level commitment to AI transformation.

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive recruitment posting

Why powerful: Creation of a dedicated C-suite role for AI indicates structural commitment and budget authority. This role exists only where AI is viewed as strategic priority, not experiment.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did you create your CAIO/AI leadership role, and what's their mandate?
  • 🔍 What board oversight exists for your AI transformation program?
  • 🔍 How is the AI leader positioned relative to CTO and CDO?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-First Public Mandate

What it is: CEO or board has explicitly stated AI-first or AI transformation in public communications.

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, annual report letter, CEO LinkedIn post

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability. When CEOs say AI is strategic in front of investors, they must deliver. This forces resource allocation and executive attention.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Can you share what the CEO's public AI mandate looks like?
  • 🔍 What's the timeline attached to your AI transformation commitment?
  • 🔍 How is AI performance measured at executive level?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board Pressure / Board Directors Talking About AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy, AI initiatives appearing in board materials, directors publicly discussing AI.

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM discussions, board committee creation for AI

Why powerful: Board pressure is the most powerful forcing function in enterprise. When directors ask about AI, CEOs must act. This bypasses internal politics and accelerates spending.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How frequently does AI appear on your board agenda?
  • 🔍 What board-level AI governance structures exist?
  • 🔍 Are directors receiving AI education or briefings?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI Mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with track record of AI transformation or who has publicly signaled AI as priority.

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO vision statement

Why powerful: New CEOs have board backing, no legacy system protection, and mandate to transform. They're evaluated on transformation outcomes, making them high-probability buyers.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the new CEO's background with AI transformation?
  • 🔍 What did the board mandate the new CEO to deliver on AI?
  • 🔍 How is the leadership team being restructured for AI?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI Platform Team (Building Not Buying)

What it is: 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure including RAG systems, agents, embeddings pipelines, custom models.

Examples: Engineering blog posts, job postings for AI platform roles, LinkedIn engineer activity, tech conference talks

Why powerful: Companies building internal AI platforms are making significant bets. They've moved beyond experimentation to production AI, indicating substantial budget and serious intent.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's your internal AI platform architecture look like?
  • 🔍 How many engineers are dedicated to AI infrastructure?
  • 🔍 What AI capabilities are you building vs buying?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI Talent Hiring Spree

What it is: 5+ AI-specific hires in 6 months including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers, data scientists.

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, recruitment campaigns, talent acquisition press releases

Why powerful: Hiring sprees indicate scaling phase. Multiple AI hires suggest proven ROI from initial experiments and commitment to expansion.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving your recent AI talent acquisition?
  • 🔍 What's your AI team headcount growth over 12 months?
  • 🔍 Which AI roles have been hardest to fill?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process Redesign Evidence

What it is: Evidence of eliminated old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes.

Examples: Company blog posts, case studies, press mentions, internal change communications, operational announcements

Why powerful: Process redesign proves AI is driving actual business change, not just tech upgrades. This indicates transformation maturity and measurable ROI expectations.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which core processes have you redesigned for AI?
  • 🔍 What's the operational impact of those process changes?
  • 🔍 How are you measuring process efficiency gains?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI Integrator Partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators on implementation rather than just buying SaaS tools.

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, system integrator relationships, implementation partner logos

Why powerful: Engaging integrators indicates scale beyond point solutions. Companies ready for enterprise transformation invest in implementation partnerships.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which AI implementation partners are you working with?
  • 🔍 What's the scope of your integrator engagement?
  • 🔍 What capabilities are you building internally vs partnering?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI Budget Increase

What it is: 50%+ year-over-year increase in AI budget allocation.

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, internal budget communications, CFO announcements

Why powerful: Doubling AI budget signals strategic commitment. Budget growth typically follows proof of ROI, making this a strong qualification signal.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving your AI budget increase this year?
  • 🔍 How is your AI budget allocated across build vs buy?
  • 🔍 What's your AI spend as percentage of revenue?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government Policy Signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements that affect the company's sector, creating regulatory or market urgency.

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Government action creates urgency and forces response. Policy shifts often open funding and create competitive pressure, accelerating purchase decisions.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is recent government AI policy affecting your sector?
  • 🔍 Are you responding to regulatory requirements with AI investment?
  • 🔍 What government AI incentives or grants are you accessing?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry Disruption Indicators

What it is: Company's sector undergoing AI-driven disruption through new entrants, margin compression, or customer behavior shifts.

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, industry analyst coverage, market research, competitive landscape changes

Why powerful: Disruption creates urgency. When analysts and press focus on industry transformation, companies must respond or risk obsolescence. This context prioritises BD conversations.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is AI disrupting your specific sector right now?
  • 🔍 What are your competitors doing with AI that's winning market share?
  • 🔍 What's the cost of inaction on AI in your industry?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI Moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives, gaining market share through AI-enabled products or services.

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, analyst coverage of competitor AI

Why powerful: Competitor success creates FOMO and urgency. When peers win with AI, transformation becomes defensive necessity, not just strategic option.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Which competitor AI initiatives concern you most?
  • 🔍 What market share are competitors gaining through AI?
  • 🔍 How are you benchmarking against competitor AI capabilities?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

New AI Features Announced

What it is: Product updates adding AI features without broader organizational transformation.

Examples: Product press releases, feature announcements, app updates

Why powerful: Low precision signal. Feature shipping is marketing, not transformation. Doesn't indicate budget or organizational readiness for enterprise AI.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Is this a product feature or broader transformation initiative?
  • 🔍 What's driving the AI feature investment vs core platform?
  • 🔍 How does product AI tie to enterprise strategy?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

AI Training Programs

What it is: Internal upskilling programs without evidence of process change or operational deployment.

Examples: Training announcements, learning program launches, certification programs

Why powerful: Low precision signal. Training without deployment is compliance or awareness, not transformation. Many companies train without implementing.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Where is trained talent being deployed?
  • 🔍 What's the deployment rate of trained AI skills?
  • 🔍 Are you training for adoption or just awareness?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

AI Task Force / Committee

What it is: Planning bodies without evidence of investment or execution.

Examples: Committee announcements, working groups, strategy task forces, planning initiatives

Why powerful: Low precision signal. Task forces often indicatepreparation, not commitment. Many companies plan without budgets or timelines.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the task force's budget and timeline?
  • 🔍 What decisions has the committee made vs planned?
  • 🔍 Are there executive sponsors with decision authority?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

Single AI Hire

What it is: Addition of one AI role without structural change or team growth.

Examples: Single job posting, one hire announcement

Why powerful: Low precision signal. One hire could be experimental, replacement, or compliance. Doesn't indicate transformation intent.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Is this a new role or replacement?
  • 🔍 What's the hiring team's broader AI strategy?
  • 🔍 How does this role connect to business outcomes?
SIGNAL WEAK SIGNAL

AI Mentioned in Annual Report

What it is: AI referenced in regulatory filings without specific initiatives or budgets.

Examples: Annual report sections, 10-K mentions, governance disclosures

Why powerful: Low precision signal. Regulatory mentions are often box-ticking, not commitment. Almost every company mentions AI now.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the substance behind the mention?
  • 🔍 Is there budget attached to the mentioned initiatives?
  • 🔍 Who owns the AI strategy referenced?
