Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically for AI signals formalised AI strategy and executive-level commitment to transformation.

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive team org chart update

Why powerful: Creation of a C-suite role for AI indicates board-backed commitment and dedicated budget. This role exists to drive transformation - they need external partners.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is your organisation structured to execute on AI strategy? Do you have dedicated AI leadership?
  • 🔍 What's your current AI governance model and who's accountable for transformation outcomes?
  • 🔍 Are you looking for strategic partners to support your AI leadership team?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO or board has explicitly stated AI as priority in public communications, signalling strategic intent at the highest level.

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, annual report letter, CEO LinkedIn post

Why powerful: Public mandates create organisational pressure to deliver. Executions will follow statements - this creates BD urgency and buying intent.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 I saw the CEO's comments about AI transformation - what's the timeline for translating that vision into execution?
  • 🔍 How is the board measuring progress against the AI mandate? What's the governance look like?
  • 🔍 What external capabilities are you bringing in to accelerate delivery against that public commitment?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure / Board directors talking about AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy appearing in board materials, AGM discussions, or director public commentary.

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussion minutes, board committee charters adding AI

Why powerful: Board pressure forces executive action. When directors are asking about AI, CEOs must respond. This creates budget and timeline urgency.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving the board's interest in AI right now? What are they asking for?
  • 🔍 How is the board tracking AI transformation progress? What KPIs matter to them?
  • 🔍 What's the board's risk appetite for AI adoption - are they encouraging bold moves or cautious steps?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with track record of AI transformation or who has signalled AI as priority in their mandate.

Examples: CEO announcement, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO vision statement

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and no legacy systems to protect. They often bring fresh budgets and seek external partners.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's the new CEO's vision for AI and how does it differ from previous strategy?
  • 🔍 Does the CEO have a trusted team in place or are they building capability from scratch?
  • 🔍 What's the CEO's tolerance for risk in AI adoption - are they looking to move fast?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team (building not buying)

What it is: 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure including RAG systems, agents, embeddings pipelines, or custom ML platforms.

Examples: Engineering blog posts, LinkedIn hiring for ML infrastructure roles, GitHub activity, job postings for platform engineers

Why powerful: Building an internal platform signals serious investment and technical ambition. These teams need strategic guidance and cannot build everything alone.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's your current AI platform architecture and where are the biggest gaps?
  • 🔍 How are you thinking about scale - build vs buy vs partner for your AI infrastructure?
  • 🔍 What does your AI engineering team need most right now to accelerate delivery?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: Significant surge in AI-specific hires including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers, data scientists within 6 months.

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge on company careers page, 5+ AI roles advertised in 6 months

Why powerful: Hiring at scale indicates budget approval and organisational commitment. Growing teams need strategy, process, and partner support.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's driving the recent AI hiring surge? What's the headcount trajectory?
  • 🔍 How quickly do you need to scale that team and what's the biggest challenge in finding the right talent?
  • 🔍 Are you building to support a specific transformation program or general capability building?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Evidence of eliminated old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes, not just tool implementation.

Examples: Company blog case studies, internal process documentation, press mentions of workflow transformation, efficiency announcements

Why powerful: Process change而非产品功能 - this signals actual transformation, not experimentation. Real change requires strategic partners.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What processes have you redesigned for AI and what's the impact?
  • 🔍 How are you managing change management alongside the technical implementation?
  • 🔍 Are you looking to scale successful pilots across more processes?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators on implementation, not just buying standalone SaaS tools.

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, system integrator engagement announcements

Why powerful: Engaging integrators shows willingness to invest in transformation, not just tools. Indicates budget and program maturity.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What AI partners are you working with currently and what's the scope of engagement?
  • 🔍 How do you evaluate external AI consultants - what matters most in a partner?
  • 🔍 What's your model for combining internal capability with external partners?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: 50%+ year-over-year increase in AI budget, indicating prioritisation and board-level commitment to AI investment.

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, internal budget approvals, CFO commentary on AI investment

Why powerful: Budget increases create execution pressure. Money must be deployed - this creates BD timing opportunity.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How was that budget increase allocated and what's the spending timeline?
  • 🔍 What's driving the increased investment - new use cases, scale, or regulatory requirements?
  • 🔍 What does success look like for that budget - what ROI are you targeting?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements that affect the company's sector, creating urgency to respond.

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI mandates

Why powerful: Policy changes create urgency, force companies to respond, and often open funding or compliance requirements that accelerate buying.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is regulatory change affecting your AI strategy? Is it driving urgency?
  • 🔍 What compliance requirements are you preparing for and what's the timeline?
  • 🔍 Are you engaging with policy consultations or industry bodies on AI regulation?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: Company's sector undergoing AI-driven disruption through new entrants, margin compression, or customer behaviour shifts.

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, increasing industry analyst coverage, market share shifts

Why powerful: Disruption creates strategic urgency. Companies that ignore AI risk competitive position - fear drives action.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How do you see AI changing your industry over the next 3-5 years?
  • 🔍 What's the competitive threat from AI-native entrants in your sector?
  • 🔍 How is customer behaviour changing and what does that mean for your AI strategy?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives, gaining market share through AI, or differentiating on AI capabilities.

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, competitor case studies

Why powerful: Competitive pressure creates FOMO and urgency. When rivals move, companies must respond - this opens BD conversations.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How are your competitors approaching AI and what are you seeing in the market?
  • 🔍 What's your competitive response to AI-native disruptors?
  • 🔍 How do you differentiate your AI strategy from competitors?
