Lead Signals

BD signal framework for qualifying AI transformation prospects

SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

CAIO / Chief AI Officer created

What it is: New C-suite role specifically created for AI, indicating formal organizational commitment to AI transformation

Examples: LinkedIn announcement, press release, board filing, executive team org chart update

Why powerful: New C-suite roles require board approval and represent structural commitment. CAIO signals AI is a strategic priority, not an experiment. These leaders have budgets and mandates to drive transformation.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 When did you create your AI leadership role and what's been the focus so far?
  • 🔍 How is your CAIO positioned in the org structure — direct to CEO or board?
  • 🔍 What's the mandate and budget your AI leader has been given?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Explicit AI-first public mandate

What it is: CEO or board has explicitly stated AI as a strategic priority in public communications

Examples: Earnings call transcript, investor day presentation, press interview, CEO blog post, annual report letter

Why powerful: Public commitments create accountability. When CEOs say 'AI-first' publicly, they risk reputation if nothing happens. This forces resource allocation and organizational alignment.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 I saw your CEO mention AI transformation recently — what's the specific vision?
  • 🔍 How is that public mandate translating into execution across the business?
  • 🔍 What does 'AI-first' actually mean for your strategy in practice?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Board pressure / Board directors talking about AI

What it is: Board-level conversations about AI strategy appearing in board materials, announcements, or director LinkedIn activity

Examples: Board announcements, director LinkedIn posts about AI, AGM AI discussions, board committee charters adding AI

Why powerful: Board pressure is the most powerful forcing function in enterprise. When directors ask about AI, CEOs must respond. This creates urgency and budget that trickles down to execution teams.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is your board thinking about AI risk and opportunity?
  • 🔍 What AI topics have come up in recent board discussions?
  • 🔍 Are you feeling pressure from directors to show AI progress?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

New CEO with AI mandate

What it is: New CEO (within 12-18 months) with track record of AI transformation or who has signaled AI as priority

Examples: CEO announcement press release, incoming CEO's prior AI work, mandate letters, CEO vision statements

Why powerful: New CEOs restructure for transformation, have board backing, and no legacy systems to protect. They enter with political capital to make bold moves. AI is often their signature initiative.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's your new CEO's vision for AI transformation?
  • 🔍 How does your leadership team align on AI priorities?
  • 🔍 What changes has the new CEO made to enable AI adoption?
SIGNAL STRONG SIGNAL

Internal AI platform team (building not buying)

What it is: 10+ engineers building internal AI infrastructure including RAG systems, agents, embeddings pipelines

Examples: Engineering blog posts, LinkedIn job postings for AI engineers, tech talks, open source contributions

Why powerful: Companies building internal AI platforms are committing serious capital and expertise. They recognize AI as competitive moat, not just tool procurement. This indicates transformation ambition.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What's your internal AI engineering capability looking like?
  • 🔍 Are you building or buying your AI infrastructure?
  • 🔍 What AI platforms are your engineers currently building?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI talent hiring spree

What it is: 5+ AI-specific hires in 6 months including prompt engineers, AI architects, ML engineers

Examples: LinkedIn hiring signals, job posting surge, talent acquisition announcements

Why powerful: Hiring sprees indicate committed investment. Multiple AI roles suggest structural intent, not one-off experiments. Shows growth trajectory and budget availability.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How has your AI team grown in the past 12 months?
  • 🔍 What AI roles are you prioritizing in your hiring plan?
  • 🔍 What's driving your AI talent acquisition strategy?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Process redesign evidence

What it is: Evidence of eliminated old workflows and creation of new AI-native processes

Examples: Company blog posts, case studies, press mentions, internal documentation

Why powerful: Process change is the hallmark of real transformation, not just technology adoption. Companies redesigning workflows have moved beyond pilot thinking to operationalize AI.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What processes have you redesigned around AI capabilities?
  • 🔍 How has AI changed your way of working?
  • 🔍 What's an example of a workflow you've transformed with AI?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

AI integrator partnerships

What it is: Working with AI consultancies or system integrators (beyond just buying SaaS tools)

Examples: Partner announcements, case studies with consulting firms, system integrator engagements

Why powerful: Companies working with integrators are investing in transformation, not point solutions. Shows willingness to spend on strategy and implementation support — indicators of serious budgets.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 Who are you working with to drive your AI transformation?
  • 🔍 What does your AI partner ecosystem look like?
  • 🔍 How are you supplementing internal capability with external expertise?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Significant AI budget increase

What it is: 50%+ year-over-year increase in AI budget

Examples: Budget filings, investor presentations, CFO statements, departmental budget approvals

Why powerful: Budget increases signal commitment. 50%+ growth suggests AI is moving from experimental to strategic. These companies have money to spend on partnerships and solutions.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How has your AI budget evolved this year?
  • 🔍 What's driving your increased AI investment?
  • 🔍 What percentage of total tech budget is AI now?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Government policy signals

What it is: Politicians or bureaucrats making AI announcements affecting the company's sector

Examples: Policy announcements, government AI strategies, regulatory changes, sector-specific AI frameworks

Why powerful: Government action creates urgency and forces companies to respond. Opens funding opportunities and compliance requirements that accelerate AI adoption. Creates timing pressure.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is government AI policy affecting your sector planning?
  • 🔍 Are there regulatory changes driving your AI timeline?
  • 🔍 What government AI initiatives are relevant to your business?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Industry disruption indicators

What it is: Company's sector undergoing AI-driven disruption from new entrants, margin compression, or customer behavior shifts

Examples: Analyst reports, trade publications, industry analyst coverage, market research

Why powerful: Disruption context explains urgency. When sectors shift, laggards lose market share. Understanding disruption pressure helps prioritize leads and frame BD conversations.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 How is AI changing the competitive dynamics in your industry?
  • 🔍 What disruption threats are you monitoring?
  • 🔍 How are your competitors using AI to gain advantage?
SIGNAL MEDIUM SIGNAL

Competitor AI moves

What it is: Key competitors announcing AI initiatives or gaining market share through AI

Examples: Competitor press releases, industry news, market share data, analyst comparisons

Why powerful: Competitor activity creates FOMO and urgency. When rivals win with AI, companies must respond. Powerful for BD framing — positions Helix as competitive necessity.

🎯 BD DISCOVERY QUESTIONS
  • 🔍 What are your key competitors doing with AI?
  • 🔍 Have you seen competitors gain advantage through AI?
  • 🔍 How are you thinking about AI relative to your competitive set?
